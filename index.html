Modern Cordel

By Milton Duarte

I’m getting kind of tired
Of all this technology
We just talk through e-mail now
Short and cold, with no warm plea
Twitter and Facebook, too
Before I’m old and through
I’ll say it all in poetry.

It’s no longer like before
Everything is shortened down
“You” now has just two letters
The “O” and “E” thrown out and gone
To declare your love today
You just send a flower away
And a drawn heart gets it done.

The “at” sign doesn’t weigh a thing
It’s part of an address
A period’s hardly used at all
Or comes at the start, no less
Now it’s all “.com” instead
If the website’s widely read
It’s worth a high-priced guess.

To make the language stranger still
The emoticon is a mark
There’s a symbol for everything now
Semicolon or asterisk spark
A kiss is now a sign you make
To know what it’s supposed to take
You must decode that scribbled arc.

I miss the days of letters
Written by one’s very hand
You’d send them out in June,
By Summer’s end they’d land
But they’d quench your longing ache
They were words you’d truly make
Lines from the heart, so grand.

Now I write and send away
It reaches in a blink of time
But when I go to have a chat
The person’s left the line
They’ve opened another app instead
And the whole wide world’s been led
By technology’s current chime.

Thankfully, when it’s time to pray
No internet is required
God hears every single one
If you doubt, you can inquire
Two colons are two knees bent
Your lips the speech instrument
And God will read what you’ve inspired.
